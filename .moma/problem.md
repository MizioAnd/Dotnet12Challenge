# Problem Definition

## Orchestration
Max Iterations: 5
Visibility: low
Model: opus

## Task Description

Imagine you are the product owner responsible for the next version of .NET (".NET 12") at Microsoft. Write a product proposal that identifies your top priority for .NET 12 and explains why. The proposal must take into account what has already been released in .NET 10 and .NET 11, and must NOT propose anything already shipped in those versions. Write the proposal to `interview_solution.md`.

## Rules & Constraints

- Must not mention or propose any feature/capability already shipped in .NET 10 or .NET 11
- Written from the perspective of a Microsoft product owner for .NET 12
- Must include clear rationale ("why now", "why this")
- Executive proposal tone — concise but substantive (not a marketing blurb, not a deep technical RFC)
- Must be structured with clear sections (e.g. vision, top priority, rationale, risks/mitigations, success metrics)
- Single top priority — do not propose a laundry list

## Review Criteria

1. Clearly identifies a single top priority for .NET 12
2. Avoids any feature/capability already shipped in .NET 10 or .NET 11
3. Provides strong, evidence-based rationale ("why now")
4. Demonstrates awareness of the .NET ecosystem trajectory and competitors
5. Aligns with Microsoft's broader strategy (Azure, AI, developer productivity)
6. Addresses concrete user/developer pain points
7. Includes risks, trade-offs, and mitigations
8. Defines measurable success metrics
9. Written in a credible product-owner voice (executive but substantive)
10. Well-structured, concise, and free of fluff
